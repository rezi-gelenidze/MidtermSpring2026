Simplified UNO Rules

This guide explains the exact game variant implemented by the CLI project. It assumes you have never played UNO before.

Core idea: on your turn, play one card that matches the card in the middle. The first player with no cards left wins.

Table Layout

Each player has their own hand of cards. The middle of the table has a draw pile and an up card. The up card is the card everyone must match.

In this CLI version, all hands are printed to the terminal on each turn. They are not hidden from the human player.

Goal

Your goal is to get rid of every card in your hand. When you play your last card, you win that game.

At the end of the game, the winner scores points from the cards still left in the other players' hands.

What You Can Play

On your turn, your card must match the up card in at least one valid way.

up card

If the up card is R5, these cards are legal:

R9

Match By Color

If the up card is red, any red card can be played.

Match By Number

If the up card is a 5, any other 5 can be played.

Match By Action Type

A skip can be played on another skip. A draw two can be played on another draw two. A reverse can be played on another reverse.

Play A Wild

W and W4 can always be played. After playing one, the player chooses the next color: R/Y/G/B.

Turn Flow

  1. Look at the up card.
  2. Look at your hand. Cards are shown as index:card, such as 1:R9.
  3. Play one legal card by typing its index or card code, or type draw.
  4. If you draw a card and it is playable, a human player may play it immediately. Bot players always play a drawn card automatically when it is legal.
  5. If you play an action card, its effect happens.
  6. The turn moves to the next player.
Input detail: if you type a card code for an illegal card, the game tells you the card is not legal and asks again. If you type an index for an illegal card, the game treats it as a bad move: you draw a penalty card and lose your turn.
You may type draw on any human turn, even if you have a card you could legally play. The game does not enforce a must-play rule.

Action Cards

Skip

Examples: RS, YS, GS, BS

The next player loses their turn.

Reverse

Examples: RR, YR, GR, BR

The direction of play reverses. With 2 players, reverse effectively skips the other player because reversing direction and advancing one step lands back on the same player.

Draw Two

Examples: R+2, Y+2, G+2, B+2

The next player draws 2 cards and loses their turn.

Wild

Code: W

The player chooses the next color.

Wild Draw Four

Code: W4

The player chooses the next color. The next player draws 4 cards and loses their turn.

Winning And Scoring

The first player to play their last card wins the game. When a player has one card left, the game prints an UNO message automatically.

Draw Pile

When the draw pile becomes empty, the discard pile is shuffled back into the draw pile.

If both the draw pile and discard pile are empty, this simplified implementation creates a W fallback card. This is mainly a safety behavior for unusual edge cases and should not normally happen in regular play.

Safety Limit

The game stops after 3000 turns if no player has won. If this happens, the CLI prints Game stopped at safety limit. This is a guard against endless games during testing.

Not Implemented

This is a simplified teaching version. It does not implement every official or house rule.

  • challenging Wild Draw Four
  • penalty for failing to say UNO
  • stacking draw cards
  • jump-in
  • 7-0 rule
  • official target score, such as 500
  • forcing a human player to play a card they just drew
  • multiple human players in one game
  • saved match configuration
